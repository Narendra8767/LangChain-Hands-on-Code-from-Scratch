AB de Villiers (Abraham Benjamin de Villiers) is one of the most celebrated and versatile cricketers in the history of the game. Here are some key highlights about him:

Career Highlights:
Nickname: Mr. 360 (for his ability to play shots all around the ground)

Born: February 17, 1984, in Pretoria, South Africa

Role: Batsman (Middle-order), Wicketkeeper (occasionally)

Teams: South Africa (International), Royal Challengers Bangalore (IPL), Titans (Domestic), and various T20 leagues worldwide.

International Career (South Africa):
Debut: 2004 (Test) vs England, 2005 (ODI) vs England, 2006 (T20I) vs Australia.

Formats Played: Tests, ODIs, T20Is.

Records:

Fastest ODI century (31 balls) vs West Indies (2015).

Fastest ODI fifty (16 balls) vs West Indies (2015).

Over 20,000 international runs (Tests, ODIs, T20Is combined).

Holds the record for the fastest Test century by a South African (75 balls vs India, 2010).

IPL & T20 Leagues:
Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB): Played from 2011-2021, forming a legendary partnership with Virat Kohli.

Scored over 5,000 IPL runs.

Known for his explosive batting, including a memorable 129* (52 balls) vs Gujarat Lions (2016).

Retirement & Comeback:
Retired from international cricket in 2018 but continued playing T20 leagues.

Made a brief comeback in 2021 for South Africa but retired again.

Officially retired from all forms of cricket in November 2021.

Legacy:
Regarded as one of the most innovative and destructive batsmen ever.

Exceptional fielder and a handy wicketkeeper.

Known for his sportsmanship, humility, and love for the game.
